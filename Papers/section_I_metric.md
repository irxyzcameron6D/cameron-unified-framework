## I.1 The Starting Point

Standard physics describes spacetime with a 4D Minkowski metric
of signature (−,+,+,+). This captures time and three spatial
dimensions. It does not explain why gravity and electromagnetism
are separate forces, why gravity is 10⁴⁰ times weaker than EM,
or why mass and charge are different kinds of thing.

The Cameron framework starts from a different geometry: a
**6D pseudo-Euclidean metric** operating on a space with three
real dimensions and three imaginary dimensions. The metric is:

```
d  =  √[ α·(Δx_re)²  +  β·(Δx_im)² ]                    (I.1)
```

where:
- Δx_re is displacement in the real (observable) sector
- Δx_im is displacement in the imaginary (gravitational) sector
- α and β are metric operators (not free parameters — see I.3)

This single equation contains all four fundamental forces
as different projections onto different sectors at different
scales.

## I.2 The Dual Hourglass Topology

The geometry of the metric is a **dual hourglass** — two cones
joined at a central point (0,0):

```
          Real outer boundary (+∞)
               /        \
        Real sector    Imaginary sector
        (observable)   (gravitational field)
               \        /
    ────────── (0,0) ──────────  ← null annihilation surface
               /        \
        Real sector    Imaginary sector
        (anti-universe) (virtual/inner)
               \        /
          Real inner boundary (−∞ = +∞)
```

Each cone contains both real and imaginary regions.
The (0,0) point is the **null annihilation surface** — the
topological knot where real and imaginary sectors meet.
This surface is what we call a particle.

### The four regions

| Region | Metric character | Physical interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Real outer cone | α·(Δx_re)² > β·(Δx_im)² | Observable universe |
| Imaginary outer cone | β·(Δx_im)² > α·(Δx_re)² | Gravitational/quantum field |
| Real inner cone | α·(Δx_re)² > β·(Δx_im)², inverted | Anti-universe |
| Imaginary inner cone | β·(Δx_im)² > α·(Δx_re)², inverted | Virtual/entanglement region |

### The null geodesic

The boundary between real-dominated and imaginary-dominated
regions is the **null geodesic**, where d² = 0:

```
α·(Δx_re)²  =  −β·(Δx_im)²
```

This requires β < 0 (pseudo-Euclidean signature) for the
equation to have real solutions. On the null geodesic:
- d = 0 — zero metric distance between emission and absorption
- This is the photon's worldline
- The photon exists in the plane orthogonal to its direction
  of motion — zero distance ahead, zero time between emission
  and absorption

The null geodesic appears at every scale:
- At 10⁻¹⁵ m: the proton confinement surface
- At 10⁻¹⁰ m: the atomic ionization radius
- At 10²² m: the galactic Jeans radius
- At 10²⁶ m: the Hubble horizon (Cameron 2018 evanescence horizon)

**All of these are the same geometric object** — the null geodesic
of the pseudo-Euclidean metric — at different scales.

## I.3 The Cross-Tether Constraint

The metric operators α and β are not free parameters. They are
constrained by the **cross-tether relation**:

```
α/β  =  (m_e/m_p)²  =  2.966 × 10⁻⁷                     (I.2)
```

where m_e is the electron mass and m_p is the proton mass.

This single ratio:
- Sets the relative weight of real vs imaginary sector
- Determines why gravity is 10⁴⁰ times weaker than EM
- Is not a free parameter — it is derived from the requirement
  that the metric be self-consistent at the null geodesic

The ratio (m_e/m_p)² = 2.966×10⁻⁷ means the real sector is
weighted ~3.4 million times more strongly than the imaginary
sector. Electromagnetism operates primarily in the real sector.
Gravity operates primarily in the imaginary sector. Their ratio
of coupling strengths reflects the ratio of metric operators.

**The weakness of gravity is not a mystery requiring a hierarchy
problem solution. It is the ratio of metric operators, set by
the cross-tether constraint.**

## I.4 The Dual Charge at Each Point

At any point r in this metric, the inverse square law produces
two values of force — one for each sector — with opposite signs:

```
At r_re (real displacement):    F = +Q²/r_re²   [repulsive for like Q]
At r_im (imaginary displacement): F = −Q²/r_im²  [attractive for like Q]
```

The sign flip occurs because β < 0 makes d² negative for
imaginary displacements, which makes 1/d² negative, which
makes F attractive even for same-sign charges.

**This is the origin of gravitational attraction.**

It is not a separate force law. It is the same inverse square
law operating in the imaginary sector of the metric, where
the signature flip makes attraction automatic.

An observer in the real sector sees:
- Same-sign real charges: repel
- Same-sign imaginary charges: attract (gravity)
- Cross terms: van der Waals residual

An observer hypothetically in the imaginary sector would see
everything flipped — gravity would repel and EM would attract.
This is the anti-universe in the inner cone.

## I.5 The Metric Inversion at Boundaries

At the null geodesic surface (d² = 0), the metric **inverts**:
real and imaginary sectors swap roles.

Inside the proton (r < r_proton):
- The imaginary sector becomes dominant
- What was the electric force (real sector outside) becomes
  the strong force (imaginary sector inside)
- Same charge magnitude, same 1/r² law, opposite sign
- This is quark confinement from metric inversion

Outside the proton (r > r_proton):
- The real sector is dominant
- Electric charges repel (same sign in real sector)
- This is asymptotic freedom — quarks repel at short range
  relative to the inverted metric inside

The transition between these two regimes IS the confinement
boundary. No color charge, no gluons, no additional postulates.
Just the geometry of the null geodesic surface.

## I.6 The Two Conjugation Operations

The pseudo-Euclidean metric introduces two distinct conjugation
operations that do not exist in standard 4D Minkowski spacetime.

**Operation 1: Metric conjugate (d → d*)**  
Maps the real sector to the imaginary sector and vice versa.
This is the symmetry that connects the outer cone to the inner cone.
Physical meaning: maps a particle to its inner-cone partner
(negative gravitational mass).

**Operation 2: Wavefunction conjugate (Ψ → Ψ*)**  
The standard complex conjugation of quantum mechanics.
Maps a+bi → a−bi in the wavefunction.
Produces real expectation values via ⟨Ψ|O|Ψ⟩.
**This operation is unchanged from standard QM.**

The existence of two conjugation operations — rather than one —
is what allows the framework to describe both quantum correlations
(through the imaginary inner cone) and classical gravity (through
the imaginary outer cone) without contradiction.

## I.7 What the Metric Is Not

To avoid confusion with other 6D frameworks:

**This is not Kaluza-Klein theory.**
Kaluza-Klein adds compact extra dimensions to recover EM from 5D
gravity. The Cameron metric adds imaginary dimensions to a
pseudo-Euclidean space — the imaginary dimensions are not compact
and are not curled up. They are the gravitational sector.

**This is not string theory.**
String theory requires 10 or 26 dimensions for consistency.
The Cameron metric requires exactly 6 — 3 real + 3 imaginary —
determined by the requirement that the cross-tether constraint
α/β = (m_e/m_p)² produce the correct force hierarchy.

**This is not a Wick rotation.**
A Wick rotation replaces t → it to convert Minkowski to
Euclidean spacetime. The Cameron metric is not obtained by
Wick-rotating standard spacetime — it is a genuinely different
geometric structure where the imaginary dimensions carry
physical degrees of freedom (gravitational charge).
