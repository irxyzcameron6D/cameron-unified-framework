## V.1 What a Black Hole Is in This Framework

A black hole is a region where enough mass has been compressed
that the null geodesic surface — the boundary between real and
imaginary sectors — expands to enclose the compressed region.

In standard GR, the event horizon is defined by escape velocity
equaling c. In the Cameron framework it is defined by:

```
d²  =  α·(Δx_re)²  +  β·(Δx_im)²  =  0                  (V.1)
```

at the Schwarzschild radius r_s = 2GM/c².

**The event horizon IS the null geodesic surface** of the
pseudo-Euclidean metric, expanded to macroscopic scale by
the concentrated mass.

Inside this surface, the metric inverts — exactly as it inverts
inside the proton, but at a vastly larger scale. What was the
imaginary sector (gravitational) outside becomes the real sector
inside. What was the real sector (electromagnetic) outside
becomes the imaginary sector inside.

## V.2 Inside the Black Hole — The Inverted Metric

Outside (r > r_s): real sector dominant, standard physics.  
At r = r_s: d² = 0, the null geodesic, the event horizon.  
Inside (r < r_s): imaginary sector dominant, inverted physics.

**What this means for matter falling in:**

A particle falling through the event horizon crosses the null
geodesic. Its real PMV (P_re) and imaginary PMV (P_im) swap
their roles. What was kinetic energy in the real sector
becomes kinetic energy in the imaginary sector. The particle
continues to exist — it just exists in the inverted metric.

This is directly analogous to a quark crossing the proton
confinement surface. The quark does not cease to exist at
the null geodesic — it continues, but in the inverted metric
where the force signs are flipped.

## V.3 No Singularity — The Pauli Floor

Standard GR predicts a singularity at r = 0 inside a black hole
— a point of infinite density where the equations break down.
The Cameron framework predicts no singularity.

**The argument:**

Inside the black hole (inverted metric), the imaginary sector
is now the real sector. The Pauli exclusion principle, which
normally prevents electron collapse in atoms, operates in this
inverted metric on the **imaginary sector** of fermion states.

Outside: Pauli operates in real sector → electrons cannot
collapse onto nuclei → atoms exist.

Inside: Pauli operates in imaginary sector → imaginary-sector
fermion states cannot collapse → **there is a floor.**

The density cannot reach infinity because the imaginary sector
has a minimum quantum volume set by ℏ, exactly as the real
sector has a minimum quantum volume for electrons in atoms.

The interior of a black hole is a **void pressure sink**
(the tornado/hurricane analogy from the handoff) — not a
singularity. Matter piles up at the imaginary Pauli floor
and cannot compress further.

## V.4 Information Preservation — Holographic from Topology

The information paradox: does information falling into a black
hole get destroyed?

**The Cameron answer:** No. Information is encoded on the
null geodesic surface (the event horizon) by the topology
of the metric boundary.

The argument is geometric rather than dynamical:

The null geodesic surface d² = 0 is the boundary between the
real and imaginary sectors. Any physical state crossing this
boundary must leave its imprint on the boundary — exactly
as a 3D object casts a 2D shadow. The boundary is the
**holographic screen** of the system.

This is the holographic principle (Bekenstein, Hawking) derived
from topology rather than thermodynamics. The Bekenstein-Hawking
entropy S = A/(4G) (where A is the horizon area) follows from
the Cameron framework because:
- Information is encoded on the null geodesic boundary (area A)
- The encoding density is set by the minimum metric grain
  (1 bit per Planck area ~ G/c³)
- S = A·c³/(4Gℏ) = A/(4G) in natural units ✓

## V.5 Hawking Radiation — The Metric Boundary Thermal Bath

In standard QM, virtual particle pairs are constantly created
and annihilated near the event horizon. One particle falls in,
the other escapes as Hawking radiation.

In the Cameron framework:

The null geodesic surface d² = 0 is a thermal boundary between
two metric sectors at different temperatures. The real sector
outside the black hole and the inverted metric inside are not
at the same thermodynamic temperature — the metric inversion
itself creates a temperature differential.

The Hawking temperature:
```
T_H  =  ℏc³ / (8πGMk_B)                                  (V.2)
```

emerges as the temperature of the null geodesic boundary,
determined by the rate at which the PMV rotation frequency
changes across the metric inversion.

For stellar-mass black holes (M ~ M_sun): T_H ~ 10⁻⁸ K
(completely negligible — black holes this size gain mass from
the CMB faster than they radiate).

For microscopic black holes (M ~ m_Planck): T_H ~ T_Planck
(the metric boundary becomes thermodynamically dominant).

## V.6 The Bounce — Connection to Cosmology

The Cameron framework predicts that black holes do not
persist forever. The Pauli floor in the imaginary sector
provides a minimum volume. As the black hole accumulates
mass and radiates via Hawking radiation, two scenarios
compete:

**Scenario 1 (stellar mass BH):** Hawking radiation is
negligible. The BH grows by accretion until the galaxy
runs out of gas. When the last gas is consumed and the
Jeans mass condition is exceeded, the BH has no further
accretion source. Over extremely long timescales it
radiates away via Hawking radiation.

**Scenario 2 (the bounce):** When the imaginary Pauli floor
is reached — when the interior density saturates the maximum
allowed by imaginary-sector Pauli exclusion — the interior
cannot compress further. The accumulated mass-energy at the
floor represents a configuration analogous to the pre-Big Bang
state. A bounce occurs: the interior rebounds into a new
metric region.

This connects to the Cameron cosmological model (Cameron 2018):
the Big Bang was a bounce of a prior collapsed universe. Each
black hole is a potential seed for a new universe. The physical
constants of each new universe are set by the conditions at
the bounce point — providing a natural selection mechanism
for universes hospitable to structure formation.

## V.8 The Reset Condition

The Cameron cosmological model predicts a **reset condition**
— the state from which a bounce originates:

1. All gas consumed by star formation and black holes
2. All stars evolve off the main sequence
3. Black holes merge into a small number of supermassive BHs
4. Hawking radiation slowly reduces BH mass over ~10¹⁰⁰ years
5. Final BH reaches Pauli floor in imaginary sector
6. Bounce: new metric region created from imaginary floor state
7. Class fragmentation at (0,0) bounce point:
   - Class 1: micro BHs (wiped by neutrino lasing)
   - Class 2: medium fragments (first galaxy seeds → JWST anomaly)
   - Class 3: large slow fragments (lensing voids)

The Stern-Gerlach sorting of matter/antimatter by the rotating
core magnetic field at the bounce moment produces the observed
matter-antimatter asymmetry of the observable universe.
